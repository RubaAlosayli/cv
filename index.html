Section 03 · Method

Design isn't taste. It's decisions.

A short blacklist of design habits I avoid — and the decisions that replace them.
Habits I avoid Decisions I make
  1. ✕ "This design feels nice." — a decision based on personal taste
    ✓ "I choose design based on principles." — clarity, hierarchy, purpose
  2. ✕ "I design a single screen." — moment-based thinking
    ✓ "I build a system, not a screen." — scalable, reusable, structured
  3. ✕ "Users will figure it out." — assumption without validation
    ✓ "I test before I defend." — data-driven decisions
  4. ✕ "Make it smart and impressive." — unnecessary complexity
    ✓ "I choose clarity." — if it needs explanation, it's a problem
  5. ✕ "We need to finish this quickly." — speed over quality
    ✓ "I design with intention, not urgency." — thoughtful execution

Better decisions → Better design.

— class dismissed.
